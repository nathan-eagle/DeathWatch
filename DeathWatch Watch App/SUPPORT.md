# Death-Watch Support

Thank you for using Death-Watch, a philosophical countdown app for Apple Watch. This document provides support information and answers to common questions.

## Contact Information

For support inquiries, please:

- Create an [issue](https://github.com/nathan-eagle/DeathWatch/issues) on this repository
- Email: support@aerieventures.com
- Response time: Usually within 48 hours

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Installation & Setup

**Q: How do I install Death-Watch on my Apple Watch?**  
A: Download Death-Watch from the App Store and it will automatically install on your paired Apple Watch. You can also install directly from the Watch App Store.

**Q: What Apple Watch models are supported?**  
A: Death-Watch works on all Apple Watch models running watchOS 11.2 or later, including Apple Watch SE, Series 4-11, and Ultra models.

**Q: How do I set my target date?**  
A: Open the app on your Apple Watch, then tap "Change Death Date" and use the digital crown to set your desired date.

### Widget & Complications

**Q: How do I add the Death-Watch complication to my watch face?**  
A: Long-press on your current watch face, tap "Edit," and add the Death-Watch complication to your preferred position.

**Q: Why isn't my widget updating?**  
A: Widgets refresh periodically to conserve battery. Force a refresh by opening the main app or restarting your watch if necessary.

**Q: Can I change which time unit (hours/days/weeks) appears on the complication?**  
A: Yes. Open the main app and tap on your preferred time unit to select it. The widget will update to display your selection.

### Troubleshooting

**Q: The app shows incorrect time remaining**  
A: Check that your watch's date and time settings are correct. If the issue persists, try restarting your watch.

**Q: Text appears cut off on my watch**  
A: Death-Watch is optimized for all watch sizes, but if text appears cut off, please report the issue with your specific watch model.

**Q: The app crashed or is behaving unexpectedly**  
A: Try these steps:
1. Restart your Apple Watch
2. Ensure you're running the latest version of watchOS
3. Reinstall the app if necessary
4. Report the issue with details about what happened

## Reporting Issues

When reporting an issue, please include:

1. Your Apple Watch model
2. watchOS version
3. Detailed description of the problem
4. Steps to reproduce the issue
5. Screenshots if possible

## Feature Requests

We welcome suggestions for improving Death-Watch. Please create an issue labeled "Feature Request" with a detailed description of your idea.

## Privacy

Death-Watch stores your target date locally on your device. No personal data is collected or transmitted. For more information, please see our [Privacy Policy](https://github.com/nathan-eagle/DeathWatch/blob/main/PRIVACY.md).
